Background

This project aims to inform or remind the public of the growing
number of happenings that the Philippine Government conceals from
the people, and to assert the importance of having the right to know
of concerns that relate to the people and securing the safety or
stability of the future of the Filipinos and/or the country.

Blog

Changing the way we see the world opens our eyes to what we need to see.

Government Accountability

Believing that a democratic country means showing your people what
they are into and what data should they need to see, the government
can make it possible for the citizens to scrutinize the accountability of
the government. How it works, where the money goes, and what the
government does for the citizens. The first step of the president's act
of transparency is by showing to the public the data that they are
entitled to see.

Democratization of Data

After the first move, the government can promote the democratization
of data or the digging of data of the government to reveal the deeper
information the citizens need. In this way, the citizens' view of the data
will widen and will give them more fruitful and significant information
about their country and the government.

Shedding Light on Corruption

Having a variety of benefits from transparency, this may be the most
important the citizens can get. We see every action of the government,
even the smallest and vaguest act of cheating or stealing. We see the
prices of oils, electricity and goods - the taxes they pay, where they go
and what good they bring - the officials they elected, what they do and
what good they give. These show how the country is progressing. It is
important for citizens to have an actual view of what happens.

Collaborative Government

Because of all the improvement that the transparency of the
government brings, the country will have a better collaboration with its
people and the countries all over the world. Because of the
truthfulness it brings to its citizens, these citizens will be the living
testimony of the memorandum's success.
